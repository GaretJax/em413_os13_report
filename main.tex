      \noindent \textbf{Challenge Topic}\\
      Currently, Airbus operates constellations of Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites that deliver imagery with an end-to-end latency ranging from approximately 49 to 149 minutes, measured from the time an acquisition plan is uploaded via S-band to the time the image is delivered and processed on the ground. This latency profile is driven by a combination of orbital dynamics, ground contact availability, data downlink processes, and downstream processing workflows. The challenge addressed in this project is to design, prototype, and demonstrate a satellite system architecture capable of reducing end-to-end data latency by an order of magnitude, from roughly one hour to single-digit minutes, without compromising mission effectiveness, data integrity, or system viability.
